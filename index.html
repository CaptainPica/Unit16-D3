Correlations Discovered Between Obesity and Poverty

What can be seen here is a further example of the poverty cycle in action. For those unaware, the poverty cycle is the inability of someone in poverty to escape poverty without help from some outside factor.

Obesity has long been linked to reduced energy, worse emotional state, and greatly increased health risks. People in poverty are more likely to be obese. This in turn increases the likelihood of expensive medical bills later on.

People who are struggling to pay off debts would struggle to take the time, energy, and most importantly the money to improve their health when they have so much on their plate already.
